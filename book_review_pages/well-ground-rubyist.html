The Well-Grounded Rubyist, Second Edition addresses both newcomers to Ruby as well as Ruby programmers who want to deepen their understanding of the language. This beautifully written and totally revised second edition includes coverage of features that are new in Ruby 2.1, as well as expanded and updated coverage of aspects of the language that have changed. Purchase of the print book includes a free eBook in PDF, Kindle, and ePub formats from Manning Publications.

"Great intro to Ruby" by Kevin Sweeney - June 7, 2021

Just switched jobs from a python shop to a Ruby on Rails shop & this book is helping me come up to speed on Ruby's syntactic sugar, of which there is arguably too much. Like, I don't want this much sugar; can you put in fewer pumps of the Torani flavor syrup, please? I like my iced coffee beverage on the bitter side, like my soul. Anyway, back to the book. It's doing a fine job of rounding out my understanding of unfamiliar syntactical patterns as well as some of the more subtle gotchas like the difference between dup vs clone (hint: freeze!). You have to know to ask a question to find something on StackOverflow and I definitely don't know all the right questions to be asking.

"A good introductory book for Ruby" by Honest Feedback - February 3, 2017

I bought this book for a class I attended at a local, junior college. A good introductory book for Ruby. If you have no prior programming experience, a concept or two might be challenging to understand. I say that because the book does cover a lot of basic, programming concepts, but there are only a few exercises per chapter, which are mostly demonstrative and only scratches the surface. The exercises work, but I prefer a lot of examples so I can practice.
